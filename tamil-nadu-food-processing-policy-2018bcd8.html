    Tamil Nadu Food Processing Policy, 2018

    Website write up

    Introduction:

     

    As a part of our commitment to fostering economic growth, promoting sustainable practices, and enhancing the overall well-being of our citizens, the government of Tamil Nadu is proud to introduce the Food Processing Policy with a range of incentives and subsidies. This policy aims to encourage investment, innovation, and job creation in the food processing sector within the state of Tamil Nadu.

    1.  Financial Incentives:

    • Capital Subsidy: The state will provide a capital subsidy on eligible investments in setting up new food processing units or expanding existing ones.
    • Interest Subsidy: Entrepreneurs engaging in food processing activities will be eligible for an interest subsidy on loans taken for establishing or modernizing their units.

    2.  Tax Benefits:

    • Goods and Services Tax (GST) Benefits: Food processing units will be eligible for specific GST benefits and exemptions as per the state's policy to reduce the overall tax burden. 
    • Income Tax Exemptions: Eligible food processing enterprises will enjoy income tax exemptions for a specified period, encouraging long-term sustainability and growth.

    3.   Infrastructure Support:

    • Industrial Parks and Zones: The state will establish dedicated industrial parks and zones for food processing units, equipped with state-of-the-art infrastructure to facilitate smoother operations. 
    • Common Facilities: Shared infrastructure facilities such as cold storage, testing laboratories, and waste management systems will be developed to reduce operational costs for the units.

    4.  Skill Development and Training:

    • Subsidized Training Programs: The state will subsidize skill development and training programs for workers in the food processing sector to enhance their capabilities and promote employment. 
    • Collaboration with Educational Institutions: Partnerships with educational institutions will be encouraged to develop specialized courses catering to the needs of the food processing industry.

    5.  Sustainability Initiatives:

    • Subsidies for Green Practices: Units adopting eco-friendly practices, renewable energy sources, and sustainable waste management will be eligible for additional subsidies. 
    • Certification Support: The state will assist units in obtaining relevant certifications for sustainable and organic food processing practices.

    6.  Market Access and Promotion:

    • Market Linkages: The government will facilitate market linkages for food processing units, connecting them with retailers, export markets, and other distribution channels. 
    • Promotional Campaigns: The state will initiate promotional campaigns to highlight the quality and uniqueness of food products manufactured within Tamil Nadu.

    7.  Technology Adoption:

    • Technology Subsidies: Units incorporating advanced technologies in their processes will be eligible for subsidies to promote innovation and efficiency.
    • Research and Development Support: The government will encourage research and development initiatives in the food processing sector by providing financial support for relevant projects.

    By implementing these incentives and subsidies, the government of Tamil Nadu aims to create a conducive environment for the growth of the food processing industry, generate employment opportunities, and contribute to the overall economic development of the state.
